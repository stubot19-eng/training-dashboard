## 1. WEST BRANCH DELAWARE RIVER (Hale Eddy, NY)
**River Type:** Tailwater fishery (Cannonsville Reservoir)
**Prime Season:** Year-round (stable flows)

### BEST WEEKENDS 2026:
1. **April 18-19** (Opening Weekend - Trout Season Opens April 1 in NY)
   - Hendrickson hatch beginning (water temps 50-55¬∞F)
   - Pre-runoff clarity, moderate flows
   - BWOs active in afternoon

2. **May 16-17** 
   - Peak Hendrickson hatch (Ephemerella subvaria)
   - Sulphurs (Ephemerella invaria) starting
   - Water temps ideal at 55-60¬∞F

3. **June 6-7**
   - Sulphurs at peak
   - Light Cahills starting
   - Stoneflies (Little Yellow Sally) active
   - Evening spinner falls spectacular

### FLIES TO HAVE READY:
- **Hendrickson (#12-14):** March Brown style, Parachute, Comparadun
- **BWO (#18-20):** Sparkle Dun, Thorax, RS2
- **Sulphur (#14-16):** Comparadun, Sparkle Dun, Spinner
- **Stoneflies (#12-16):** Yellow Sally stimulators
- **Caddis (#16-18):** Tan/olive elk hair
- **Streamers:** Woolly Buggers, Sculpins, Zonkers (for big browns)

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- **Trophy Section:** "No-kill" area below Hale Eddy - barbless hooks only
- **Flow dependent:** Check Cannonsville release schedule (usually 200-1200 cfs)
- **Wade carefully:** Smooth, slippery rocks - felt or studded boots essential
- **Big fish:** Home to 18-24" wild browns and rainbows
- **Camping:** Cold Spring campground nearby for multi-day trips

## 2. FARMINGTON RIVER (Riverton, CT)
**River Type:** Tailwater + freestone (West Branch + main stem)
**Prime Season:** Year-round (TMA open all year)

### BEST WEEKENDS 2026:
1. **March 28-29** 
   - Early Black Stoneflies (first major hatch!)
   - Midges abundant
   - Pre-season solitude, catch & release only until Opening Day

2. **April 25-26**
   - Hendricksons in full swing
   - Blue Quills (#18) starting
   - Opening Day energy (CT opens 2nd Saturday in April)
   - Water temps perfect 52-58¬∞F

3. **May 30-31 (Memorial Day Weekend)**
   - Sulphurs at peak
   - March Browns in evening
   - Caddis hatches (#16-18)
   - Green Drake on upper river

### FLIES TO HAVE READY:
- **Early Season:** Black Stonefly (#12-14), Midges (#20-24), BWO (#18-20)
- **Hendrickson (#12-14):** Sparkle Dun, Comparadun
- **Sulphur (#14-16):** Parachute, Comparadun
- **March Brown (#10-12):** Wulff patterns
- **Caddis (#14-18):** Elk Hair, X-Caddis
- **Streamers:** Sex Dungeons, Sculpins

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- **TMA (Trout Management Area):** From Goodwin Dam to Route 177 bridge - year-round fishing
- **Broodstock Atlantic Salmon:** Present in this river!
- **Busy on weekends:** Get there early (before 7 AM)
- **Catch & Release:** Most sections require barbless, no-kill
- **Wading:** Can be challenging - bring wading staff
- **Food:** Dunkin' Donuts in Riverton for pre-dawn coffee

## 3. WALLOOMSAC RIVER (North Bennington, VT)
**River Type:** Freestone tributary (of Hoosic River)
**Prime Season:** April-October (VT trout season opens 2nd Sat in April)

### BEST WEEKENDS 2026:
1. **April 11-12**
   - Vermont Opening Day weekend!
   - Early stoneflies active
   - Midges and small BWOs (#20-22)
   - Stocked trout fresh and aggressive

2. **May 9-10**
   - Hendricksons and Blue Quills
   - Water warming to optimal 55¬∞F
   - Pre-runoff clarity still good
   - Mother's Day = lighter pressure

3. **June 13-14**
   - Sulphur hatch peak
   - Light Cahills (#12-14)
   - Golden Drakes (#8-10) in sections
   - Evening fishing excellent

### FLIES TO HAVE READY:
- **Early Season:** Blackflies (#16-18), Stoneflies (#12-14), Midges (#20-24)
- **Hendrickson (#12-14):** Dun, Spinner, Emerger
- **Sulphur (#14-16):** Parachute, Spinner
- **Caddis (#14-18):** Olive, Tan
- **Terrestrials:** Ants, Beetles (June)
- **Attractor dries:** Royal Wulff, Humpy, Stimulator

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- **Small stream:** Less pressured than major rivers
- **Brook trout:** Wild natives present in upper reaches
- **Vermont regulations:** Check special regs (some areas C&R only)
- **Nearby:** Battenkill close by - can fish both in one trip
- **Parking:** Limited in North Bennington - arrive early

## 4. BEAVERKILL RIVER (Roscoe, NY)
**River Type:** Catskill freestone (legendary status)
**Prime Season:** April-November (NY trout season)

### BEST WEEKENDS 2026:
1. **April 4-5** (Season Opener!)
   - Opening Day energy
   - Early stoneflies
   - Midges and small BWOs
   - Traditional Catskill experience

2. **May 2-3**
   - Hendricksons (Ephemerella subvaria) - THE classic Catskill hatch
   - River comes alive with rising fish
   - Red Quills (#12) spinning at dusk
   - Water temps 55-60¬∞F

3. **June 20-21**
   - Sulphurs and Light Cahills
   - Evening spinner falls legendary
   - Stoneflies (Golden) in riffles
   - Father's Day weekend tradition

### FLIES TO HAVE READY:
- **Hendrickson (#12-14):** Catskill style tied high-floating, Red Quill spinners
- **March Brown (#10-12):** Wulff, Adams variations
- **Sulphur (#14-16):** Comparadun, Sparkle Dun
- **Blue Wing Olive (#18-20):** Thorax, RS2
- **Caddis (#14-18):** Elk Hair, Henryville
- **Terrestrials:** Ants, Beetles (late June)
- **Streamers:** Muddler Minnow, Black Nose Dace

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- **Covered Bridge Pool:** Famous spot, expect company
- **Historical significance:** "The birthplace of American fly fishing"
- **Junction Pool:** Where Beaverkill meets Willowemoc - iconic
- **Famous flies:** This is where the Adams, Hendrickson, and Catskill-style dries originated
- **Roscoe (Trout Town USA):** Shops, lodging, restaurants
- **Weekends = crowds:** Fish weekdays if possible, or hike to lesser-known pools

## 5. HOUSATONIC RIVER (Cornwall, CT)
**River Type:** Larger freestone river
**Prime Season:** April-November

### BEST WEEKENDS 2026:
1. **April 11-12** (Opening Weekend)
   - Stoneflies (#12-14) early in month
   - Blue Quills (#18) starting
   - Hendricksons by mid-month
   - Flows manageable before spring runoff

2. **May 16-17**
   - Hendrickson hatch at peak
   - Caddis (#14-16) abundant
   - March Browns (#10-12) in riffles
   - Sulphurs beginning
   - Sulfur Dun (#14-16) hatches prolific

3. **June 13-14**
   - Sulphurs and Light Cahills (#12-14)
   - Golden Stones (#6-8) in faster water
   - Isonychia (Slate Drake) starting (#12)
   - Evening spinner falls excellent

### FLIES TO HAVE READY:
- **Stoneflies:** Early Black Stone (#12-14), Golden Stone (#6-10)
- **Mayflies:** Hendrickson (#12-14), Blue Quill (#18), March Brown (#10-12), Sulphur (#14-16)
- **Caddis:** Tan/Olive (#14-18), X-Caddis, Elk Hair
- **Slate Drake:** #12-14 thorax patterns
- **Terrestrials:** Ants, beetles, hoppers (late June)
- **Streamers:** Sculpins, Woolly Buggers (for big browns)

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- **Big river:** Bring 9-10' rod for better line control
- **Wild + stocked:** Mix of wild browns/brookies and stocked rainbows
- **C&R areas:** Special regulations sections - check CT DEEP website
- **Wading:** Can be challenging - higher flows than smaller streams
- **Access:** Multiple access points along Rt 7 and Rt 4
- **Scenery:** Beautiful Litchfield Hills setting

## 6. BATTENKILL RIVER (Manchester, VT)
**River Type:** Spring creek + freestone mix
**Prime Season:** April-October

### BEST WEEKENDS 2026:
1. **April 11-12** (VT Opening Day)
   - Opening weekend energy
   - Stoneflies and early BWOs
   - Fish looking up after winter
   - Midges (#20-24) still important

2. **May 9-10**
   - Hendrickson hatch (peak around Mother's Day)
   - Blue Quills (#18) active
   - Water temps ideal 55-60¬∞F
   - Beautiful Vermont spring weather

3. **May 30-31 (Memorial Day)**
   - March Browns (#10-12) in evening
   - Sulphurs (#14-16) starting
   - Caddis hatches daily
   - Fish well-educated but catchable

### FLIES TO HAVE READY:
- **Hendrickson (#12-14):** Sparkle Dun, Comparadun
- **March Brown (#10-12):** Wulff patterns, Adams
- **Blue Quill (#18):** Thorax, Comparadun
- **Sulphur (#14-16):** Parachute, Dun
- **Caddis (#14-18):** Elk Hair, Henryville Special
- **Midges (#20-24):** Griffith's Gnat, Zebra Midge
- **Terrestrials:** Ants, beetles

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- **Technical fishing:** Clear, flat water requires stealth and precise casting
- **Educated trout:** Fish have seen every fly - use light tippet (6x minimum)
- **Private land:** Much of river flows through private property - use public access points
- **Famous water:** Orvis headquarters nearby, heavily pressured
- **Trophy potential:** Big wild browns lurk in deeper pools
- **Guided trips:** Many outfitters in Manchester area
- **Permit:** Some sections require landowner permission

## 7. ESOPUS CREEK (Shandaken, NY)
**River Type:** Freestone Catskill river (turbid from reservoir releases)
**Prime Season:** April-November

### BEST WEEKENDS 2026:
1. **April 18-19**
   - Post-opening day, crowds thin
   - Stoneflies active
   - BWOs (#16-18) reliable hatch
   - Water clearing after winter

2. **May 16-17**
   - Hendricksons and March Browns
   - Caddis (#14-16) abundant
   - Best clarity of spring
   - Water temps optimal

3. **June 6-7**
   - Sulphur hatch excellent
   - Light Cahills (#12-14)
   - Golden Stones (#6-8) in pocket water
   - Wet fly fishing exceptional

### FLIES TO HAVE READY:
- **BWO (#16-18):** Thorax, Sparkle Dun
- **Hendrickson (#12-14):** Dun, Spinner, Red Quill
- **March Brown (#10-12):** Wulff patterns
- **Caddis (#14-16):** Elk Hair, Goddard
- **Stoneflies:** Golden (#6-8), Yellow Sally (#14-16)
- **Wet Flies:** March Brown, Gray Fox, Professor (traditional Catskill wets)
- **Streamers:** Black Nose Dace, Mickey Finn

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- **Reservoir releases:** Ashokan Reservoir releases can make river turbid - check release schedule
- **Best fishing:** Below Phoenicia or above reservoir
- **Turbid water:** Fish are less spooky but harder to see - fish by feel
- **Big fish:** 18-20" browns and rainbows common
- **Covered bridges:** Historic bridges add to experience
- **Access:** Multiple DEC parking areas along Rt 28
- **Camping:** DEC campgrounds nearby

## 8. RUSSELL POND/BAXTER STATE PARK (Maine)
**Water Type:** Remote pond + streams
**Prime Season:** May-September (ice-out dependent)

### BEST WEEKENDS 2026:
1. **May 23-24 (Memorial Day Weekend)**
   - Ice-out timing (typically mid-May)
   - Brook trout hungry post-winter
   - Black flies terrible but fishing incredible
   - Before summer crowds

2. **June 20-21**
   - Peak brook trout activity
   - Hexagenia hatches (#6-8) at dusk on pond!
   - Stoneflies in streams
   - Longest days = extended fishing

3. **July 4-5 Weekend**
   - Still good brook trout fishing
   - Terrestrials working well
   - Caddis hatches
   - Stable summer conditions

### FLIES TO HAVE READY:
- **Brook Trout Classics:** Royal Wulff, Adams, Hornberg
- **Hexagenia (#6-8):** Giant Mayfly spinner patterns
- **Stoneflies (#8-12):** Black, Golden
- **Caddis (#14-16):** Elk Hair
- **Attractors:** Renegade, Trude, Humpy
- **Terrestrials:** Ants, beetles, hoppers (June/July)
- **Streamers:** Black Ghost, Mickey Finn, Woolly Bugger
- **Wet Flies:** Hornberg (killer brookie fly)

### SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- **REMOTE:** 4+ hours from civilization, plan accordingly
- **Baxter State Park:** Reservation system for camping (book EARLY)
- **No bait:** Fly fishing only, artificial lures only
- **Native brook trout:** All wild, no stocking
- **Moose:** You're in moose country - keep eyes peeled
- **Black flies:** Bring head nets and DEET (May-June brutal)
- **Cell service:** None - tell someone your plans
- **Weather:** Can change rapidly - bring layers
- **Leave No Trace:** Pristine wilderness - pack out everything

# üéí ESSENTIAL GEAR CHECKLIST

## Rods:
- 9' 5wt (all-around workhorse)
- 9' 4wt (delicate dry fly presentations)
- 8'6" 3wt (small streams like Walloomsac)

## Flies by Category:
- **Dries:** Hendrickson, Sulphur, BWO, March Brown, Caddis (sizes 10-20)
- **Wets:** Traditional Catskill patterns (March Brown, Gray Fox, Professor)
- **Nymphs:** Pheasant Tail, Hare's Ear, Copper John, Zebra Midge
- **Streamers:** Woolly Buggers, Sculpins, Sex Dungeon, Mickey Finn
- **Terrestrials:** Ants, Beetles, Hoppers (late spring/summer)

## Terminal Tackle:
- 9' 4x-6x tapered leaders
- 5x-7x tippet spools
- Strike indicators
- Split shot
- Floatant
- Desiccant powder

## Wading:
- Breathable waders + wading boots
- Felt or studded soles (rocky rivers!)
- Wading staff
- Wading belt

## Accessories:
- Polarized sunglasses (essential!)
- Hat with brim
- Forceps/pliers
- Nippers
- Thermometer
- Net
- Camera/phone for grip & grins

## Baxter State Park Special Gear:
- Head net (black flies!)
- 100% DEET
- Bear spray (just in case)
- Satellite communicator (no cell service)
- Extra layers (mountain weather)
